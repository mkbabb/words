## Decision Rules (apply in order)

1. **A1**: Top ~500 words. Concrete objects, basic actions, core adjectives. Child learns by age 6.
   → cat, big, eat, house, red, go, water, mother
2. **A2**: Top ~1500. Simple descriptions, routine actions, common modifiers.
   → describe, weather, kitchen, already, foreign, gentle
3. **B1**: Top ~3500. Abstract but common concepts, workplace/school vocabulary.
   → improve, investment, personality, anxiety, estimate, threaten
4. **B2**: Top ~6000. Academic, abstract, nuanced. Requires educated reading exposure.
   → ambiguous, infrastructure, cognitive, demographic, articulate
5. **C1**: Top ~10000. Sophisticated, precise, low-frequency in speech. Common in academic writing.
   → paradigm, ubiquitous, exacerbate, juxtapose, pragmatic
6. **C2**: Beyond ~10000. Literary, archaic, or highly specialized. Native speakers may not know.
   → perspicacious, ineffable, sesquipedalian, verisimilitude, defenestration

## Tiebreakers

- If the word itself is common but this specific sense is specialized → level of the sense, not the word.
- Phrasal verbs: level of the combination, not the components (e.g., "come across" = B1, not A1).
- Prefer the lower level when genuinely ambiguous.
